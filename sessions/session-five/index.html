  • The "Flipped" Classroom

    Many of our modules use a flipped classroom pedagogy.

    The approach is to set a video to watch and/or notes to read before the lesson, where students take notes before the face-to-face class. Students bring their notes to the lesson and use them to help them complete their classwork. This means they come to the lesson with the knowledge we would have taught in class before this approach—this accelerates the pace of the lesson and covers more content in a shorter amount of time.

    A lecturer has more time to review complex concepts, ask questions, and spend time with individuals or small groups. Students will be more independent, starting the lesson with the same base knowledge, if not in their heads or books. That enables activities to begin immediately without dull "chalk and talk" time. Of course, not all students will do their homework. In that case, they watch the video in the lesson instead.
