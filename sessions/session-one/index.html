Session One - Introduction

Learning objectives

By the end of this session, you will have an understanding of

  • An overview of this course, our remote tooling, and the Department.
  • A (very) brief intro to Computer Science with "a bit" of history.
  • What the edit-compile-run cycle is, what are editors, and how helpful is an Integrated Development Environment (IDE)?
  • How computers work (straightforward overview - like the materials you will see in a programme module).

Pre-session activities

We will use the CODIO virtual coding environment for our programming sessions; you should create an account at codio. co.uk to gain access to the materials. Following the enrollment link should allow you to join the course.

If there are any issues, then please let me know (Keith Mannock)

The "Live" Session

This online session replaces what would have been our face-to-face time in a usual class. Many of our modules are "Hyflex"; which means they are available online and in-person at the same time.

The provisional session schedule is (roughly):

  • 50 minutes of me chatting
  • 10 minutes of coffee/restroom break
  • 15 minutes of demo
  • 45 minutes of you working on exercises with me online to answer any questions.
  • Session Recordings
